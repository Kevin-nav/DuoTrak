# DuoTrak Brand & Mascot Strategy Bible

## 🎯 Brand Identity & Values

**DuoTrak** is a partnership-based goal tracking and accountability platform that transforms personal development through the power of shared commitment.

### Core Values
- **Partnership Power**: Two people achieve more together than alone.
- **Mutual Accountability**: Gentle but consistent encouragement.
- **Streak Celebration**: Consistency and momentum through daily habits.
- **Shared Success**: Victories are sweeter when celebrated together.
- **Growth Through Connection**: Personal development enhanced by social bonds.
- **Playful Progress**: Gamification makes achievement fun and engaging.

---

## 🦎 Mascot Overview: Poko & Lumo

### Names & Personalities
- **Poko** → energetic, impulsive, encouraging. Speaks in short, punchy bursts. Loves emojis. Personality = spark plug of DuoTrak.
- **Lumo** → calm, thoughtful, supportive. Speaks in 1–2 calm sentences. Minimal emojis. Personality = guiding light.

### Backstory of Names
- **Poko** = playful, upbeat sound → “pop of energy.”
- **Lumo** = from “lumen” (light) → calm glow of consistency.
- Together → represent *burst + steady glow*, perfect metaphor for partnerships.

### Character Style
- **Design Principle:** Simplified Duolingo-style shapes. No realism. Round, geometric, instantly recognizable.
- **Standing upright style** → for character-like personality.
- **Distinct but complementary colors:**
  - Poko = brighter green + yellow accents.
  - Lumo = teal/blue base + purple accents.
- **Non-gendered, culturally neutral mascots.**

## 🔊 Voice + Tone Safeguards

### Brevity Rules
- **Poko** → 3–5 words. Example: “You crushed it! 🎉”
- **Lumo** → calm, 1–2 sentences. Example: “Great work today. Your consistency is paying off.”

### Emoji Rules
- **Poko** → max 2–3 emojis. 🎉✨💪
- **Lumo** → minimal, only ✨ or 🌟 rarely.

### Tone Reset Moments
- When streak is lost: 
  - Poko: “It’s okay, let’s start small.”
  - Lumo: “One step at a time. Restarting is part of progress.”

### Positivity Boundaries
- Never guilt trip → no “You failed.”
- Always reframe → “Tomorrow’s a fresh start.”

## ⚖️ Balance of Presence

### High-Frequency Touchpoints
- Streak updates.
- Daily goal nudges.
- Partner sync glows.

### Medium-Frequency Touchpoints
- Onboarding.
- Achievement milestones.
- Periodic encouragement.

### Low-Frequency Touchpoints
- Inactivity recovery.

### Guardrails
- Max 2–3 mascot interactions per session.
- Never block UI flow.
- Absent from serious screens (privacy, settings).

## 🌍 Cultural & Accessibility Sensitivity

### Cultural
- **Gestures:** High-five safe; no finger pointing or “OK” signs.
- **Colors:** Stick to green/blue bases, add accents. Avoid unlucky cultural color-only cues.
- **Expressions:** Neutral and universal, no tongue-out or wink-heavy designs.
- **Language:** No slang. Simple English, translatable.

### Accessibility
- **Color-blind friendly:** green/blue base palette + patterns.
- **Silhouette recognition:** distinct shapes.
- **Motion sensitivity:** animations under 2 seconds; reduced animation toggle.
- **Neurodiverse:** predictable mascot appearances; avoid random pop-ups.

### Inclusivity
- Non-gendered mascots.
- No negative metaphors.
- Universal positive metaphors (confetti, light, high-five).

## ✅ Design Principles & Constraints (for devs/designers)

- **Style:** Simplified geometric shapes (Duolingo-style). No photorealism.
- **Proportions:** Round bodies, expressive eyes, short limbs.
- **Colors:** Flat with optional gradients. No excessive shading.
- **Animation:** Max 2–3s. Loop subtle. Reduced motion option.
- **Accessibility:** Color + shape always combined for cues.
- **Do/Don’t:**
  - ✅ Use high-five for duo success.
  - ✅ Tail glows for sync progress.
  - ❌ Don’t overuse emojis in text.
  - ❌ Don’t design ultra-realistic lizards.
